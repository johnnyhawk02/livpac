About LivPaC

LivPaC is the official parent carer forum for Liverpool, representing families of children and young people with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND). We are a voluntary group comprised of enthusiastic, dedicated, and experienced parents and carers of children and young people aged 0-25 with all types of disabilities and additional needs.

Our Mission

"Empower parents / carers to have a voice and enable them to navigate the special educational needs and / or disabilities processes through awareness, information and confidence training"

Our collaborative approach is guided by the principle: "Alone we can do so little, but together we can do so much." Through participation and co-production, we work to foster a culture across all services that places the needs of families at the center of decision-making processes.

What We Stand For

Collaboration

Working together with local authorities, health services, and education providers to improve outcomes for children with SEND.

Support

Providing parents and carers with the information, resources and confidence they need to support their children.

Advocacy

Ensuring the voices of parents and carers are heard in the planning and delivery of SEND services in Liverpool.

Inclusivity

Promoting equal access to opportunities and resources for all children and young people with SEND in Liverpool.

Our Services and Activities

Coffee Mornings and Support Groups

We organize regular coffee mornings and meet-ups that provide opportunities for parents and carers to connect, share experiences, and offer mutual support. These sessions serve as informal spaces where families can discuss challenges, exchange practical advice, and build supportive relationships with others in similar situations.

Training and Workshops

LivPaC provides training sessions for both parents/carers and professionals working in the SEND field. These sessions cover various topics relevant to supporting children with special needs and navigating the complex systems of education, health, and social care.

One-to-One Support

For families needing more personalized assistance, we offer one-to-one support sessions at Clubmoor Children's Centre every Thursday from 10am to 2:30pm. Don't struggle alone, we're here to help!

Advocacy and Representation

A core function of LivPaC is to advocate for improved services for children and young people with SEND. We work closely with schools, health services, and local authorities to ensure that children with disabilities receive appropriate support. When gaps in service provision are identified, LivPaC collaborates with providers to enhance what is available to families in the local area.

Our Locations

LivPaC operates from several locations across Liverpool to ensure accessibility for families throughout the city:

  • Main Address: The Granby Centre, 79 Granby Street, Liverpool, L8 2TU
  • Support Sessions: Clubmoor Children's Centre on Utting Avenue East
  • Coffee Mornings: Various community venues across Liverpool

Our regular coffee morning sessions are typically held on the first Tuesday of every month at the Granby Adult Learning Centre (subject to change during school holidays).

Get Involved

There are many ways you can get involved with LivPaC and help us support Liverpool families:

  • Become a member - Join our forum and receive updates about local services, events, and consultations
  • Attend our events - Come to our coffee mornings, workshops, and information sessions
  • Share your experience - Help us gather feedback about local SEND services
  • Volunteer - Support our work by volunteering at events or helping with admin tasks
  • Spread the word - Tell other parents/carers about LivPaC and the support we offer

To find out more about getting involved, please contact us or attend one of our upcoming events.
